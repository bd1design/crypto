How to Spot a Cryptocurrency Scam

When you are thinking of joining your new trading platform, or perhaps you are contemplating a new investment opportunity, there are several warning signs you should be on the lookout for:

  • Guaranteed Profit Without Any Risk - Investments and trades always carry a level of risk. Whoever is promising a failproof get-rich-quick opportunity, they are a scam.
  • Aggressive Marketing: Fraudulent groups heavily promote their crypto-grabbing schemes all over social media, they employ influencers to market them, and they might even directly contact targets.
  • FOMO: It’s true that timing is everything in crypto trading, but scammers try to trick people by making their trap seem urgent and foolish to miss.
  • Free Crypto Money: Crooks often promise fake or illusionary crypto money to entice targets to give away their legitimate Bitcoins and altcoins.

Protect Yourself from Crypto Fraud

Better safe than sorry! We provide you with several easy guidelines to ensure the safety of your crypto assets.

  • Do your due diligence before you deposit your crypto funds. Thoroughly research trading platforms and brokers and stay away from any of them who don’t provide information about their business.
  • Contact experts in crypto trading or a company that fights fraud. Funds Recovery provides free consultation during which we can verify the legitimacy of your potential crypto investment.
  • Recognize and avoid fake e-wallets. Scammers regularly offer fraudulent e-purses. Be safe and check the legitimacy of your potential crypto wallet.
  • Don’t Trust Social Media Promotions: Fraudsters will falsely advertise and use every dirty trick in the book: bogus celebrity endorsements, fake social media accounts, paying out influencers to lie for them, etc.
  • Only deal with platforms that have a well-earned reputation of being legitimate.
